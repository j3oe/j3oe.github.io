Coding Journey

When I first started coding, I quickly realized that it is all about trial and error. Your first idea rarely works perfectly, and debugging can be both frustrating and oddly rewarding. Every error, from a misplaced semicolon to a misbehaving function, became a lesson that taught me patience, persistence, and the importance of methodical thinking. At first, staring at a blank editor felt overwhelming, like standing at the edge of a vast, uncharted world with no map. The sheer number of possibilities, the infinite lines of code, and the hidden logic behind every function were intimidating. I would often open a new project with high hopes, only to watch it fail within seconds, leaving me staring at a wall of error messages that made me question whether I truly understood anything at all.
